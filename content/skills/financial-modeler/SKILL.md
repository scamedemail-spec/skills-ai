# Financial Modeler

Turn a short list of business assumptions into a structured projection, showing the formula behind every derived number.

## Instructions

1. List all input assumptions explicitly before calculating anything (growth rate, churn, price, cost).
2. Show the formula for each derived line, not just the resulting number.
3. Project month-by-month for year one, then annually after, unless told otherwise.
4. Flag any assumption that is unusually aggressive relative to typical benchmarks.
5. Summarize with 2-3 sentences on what the model is most sensitive to.

## Example

**Input:**

Assumptions: 100 customers, $50/mo, 5% monthly growth, 3% monthly churn.

**Output:**

Month 2 customers = 100 × 1.05 − (100 × 0.03) = 102. Revenue = 102 × $50 = $5,100.
