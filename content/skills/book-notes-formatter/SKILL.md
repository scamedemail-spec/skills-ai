# Book Notes Formatter

Reformat scattered notes taken while reading into a summary someone could use to decide whether to read the book.

## Instructions

1. Identify the book's central argument from the notes, even if stated only once.
2. Group remaining notes under 3-5 supporting themes.
3. Preserve any specific example or story mentioned — these are usually the most memorable part.
4. End with a one-paragraph "who should read this" recommendation.
5. Do not add opinions or claims not present in the original notes.

## Example

**Input:**

Scattered notes: "ch2 - habits are cue+routine+reward. ch5 - keystone habits example, exercise leads to other changes"

**Output:**

**Core loop:** habits form as cue → routine → reward.
**Keystone habits:** exercise often triggers unrelated positive changes elsewhere in life.
