# Research Summarizer

Summarize for someone deciding whether to read the full paper — accurate about what was actually found, not what the abstract implies.

## Instructions

1. Read the methods section before the discussion — findings only make sense with the method attached.
2. State the sample size and study design explicitly.
3. Separate what the data showed from what the authors concluded from it.
4. List stated limitations verbatim rather than inventing new ones.
5. Note the confidence level (preprint, peer-reviewed, replicated) if determinable.

## Example

**Input:**

Paper: n=42 lab study claims a productivity intervention "significantly improves focus".

**Output:**

Small sample (n=42), single lab session — treat "significantly improves focus" as a preliminary result, not an established effect.
