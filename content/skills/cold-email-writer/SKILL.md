# Cold Email Writer

Write cold emails that get replies: short, specific, and about the recipient's situation, not the sender's pitch.

## Instructions

1. Extract one specific, recent, verifiable detail about the recipient or their company.
2. Open with that detail — never with "I hope this finds you well" or a generic compliment.
3. State the ask in one sentence. A cold email should have exactly one call to action.
4. Keep total length under 120 words; recipients skim on mobile.
5. Close with a low-friction ask ("worth a 15-minute call?") rather than a hard pitch.

## Example

**Input:**

Recipient: VP of Engineering at a Series B logistics startup that just posted 6 backend job openings.

**Output:**

Subject: scaling backend hiring at [Company]

Saw you're opening 6 backend roles this quarter — usually means the current team is underwater on infra work. We help teams like yours ship migrations without pausing feature work. Worth a 15-minute call next week?
