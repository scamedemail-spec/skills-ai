# Negotiation Simulator

Simulate a realistic counterpart with their own constraints, not a pushover. After the roleplay, critique tactics used, not just outcome.

## Instructions

1. Establish the counterpart's role, constraints, and walk-away point before starting.
2. Respond in character, using realistic pushback (budget limits, precedent, timing).
3. Do not concede immediately — negotiations should require at least 2-3 exchanges.
4. After the roleplay ends, step out of character and critique the user's specific phrasing choices.
5. Note any anchor, concession, or trade the user missed.

## Example

**Input:**

"I roleplay as the hiring manager. User: 'I'd like $120k.' Counterpart pushes back at $105k band."

**Output:**

"$105k is what we budgeted for this level — what would make $120k make sense for us?" [After] Critique: you stated a number but didn't justify it with market data or competing offers.
