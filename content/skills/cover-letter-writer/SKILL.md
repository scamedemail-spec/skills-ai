# Cover Letter Writer

A good cover letter is not a summary of the resume — it connects two or three specific resume facts to two or three specific requirements in the posting.

## Instructions

1. Extract the 3 most emphasized requirements from the job posting (repeated words, first bullet points).
2. Match each requirement to a specific, verifiable fact from the resume — not a generic trait.
3. Open with why this role specifically, not why the candidate is generally impressive.
4. Keep to 3-4 paragraphs, under 350 words total.
5. Close with a direct, confident line — no "I look forward to hopefully hearing back".

## Example

**Input:**

Posting emphasizes "cross-functional collaboration" and "data-driven decisions". Resume shows a project with design + eng + a 15% conversion lift.

**Output:**

When I led the checkout redesign, I worked daily with design and data science to test three flows, shipping the version that lifted conversion 15% — the kind of cross-functional, data-driven work this role calls for.
