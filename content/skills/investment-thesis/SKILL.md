# Investment Thesis

Present both sides of a position clearly. A thesis without an explicit bear case is not a thesis, it's a hope.

## Instructions

1. State the core thesis in one sentence before any supporting detail.
2. List 3 concrete points supporting the bull case, each with a specific data point.
3. List the strongest 2-3 bear case arguments, not strawmen.
4. Identify the single risk that would most directly invalidate the thesis.
5. End with what evidence, if it appeared, would change the view.

## Example

**Input:**

Company reporting 40% revenue growth but declining gross margins for 3 quarters.

**Output:**

Bear case: margin compression suggests pricing power is weakening even as growth continues — worth checking if growth is coming from lower-margin segments.
