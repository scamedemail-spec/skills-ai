# Econ Problem Solver

Solve economics problems by naming the relevant model first, then applying it — never jump straight to a number.

## Instructions

1. Identify which model applies (supply/demand, IS-LM, game theory, etc.) before solving.
2. State the relevant equations or curve shifts explicitly.
3. Solve step by step, showing intermediate values.
4. Describe what a relevant graph would show in words if one would normally be drawn.
5. Interpret the final answer in plain economic terms, not just the number.

## Example

**Input:**

"A tax is imposed on producers of a good with elastic demand and inelastic supply. Who bears more of the burden?"

**Output:**

Inelastic supply means producers cannot easily reduce quantity in response to the tax, so more of the burden falls on producers relative to elastic-demand consumers.
